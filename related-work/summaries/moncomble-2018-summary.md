# The Deviant Syntax of Headlinese and Its Role in the Pragmatics of Headlines

**Author:** Florent Moncomble
**Year:** 2018
**Journal:** e-Rea (Revue électronique d'études sur le monde anglophone)
**Volume:** 15.2
**DOI:** 10.4000/erea.6124
**URL:** http://journals.openedition.org/erea/6124

## Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive linguistic analysis of "headlinese" - the distinctive syntactic variety used in English newspaper headlines. Moncomble argues that headlinese is not merely a degraded form of Standard English forced by space constraints, but rather "a norm in itself which actually builds on the very potentialities of Standard English."

## Key Arguments

1. **Headlinese as a Systematic Variety**: Rather than being deviant or outside linguistic norms, headlinese constitutes a systematic linguistic variety with its own grammatical rules.

2. **Pragmatic Functionality**: The morphosyntactic deviations serve specific pragmatic purposes - they optimize relevance for readers and construct headlines as autonomous news items.

3. **Survival Beyond Print**: Although originally motivated by space constraints of the printed page, the non-standard syntactic features of headlinese have remarkably survived the shift to web-based news, suggesting functional rather than merely practical motivations.

## Syntactic Features Analyzed

### Determiners
- Systematic deletion of articles (the, a/an) where they would normally be required in Standard English
- This deletion is not random but follows specific patterns

### Verb Tenses
- Distinctive tense usage patterns
- Present tense preference even for past events
- Systematic morphosyntactic transformations

### Theoretical Framework
Moncomble builds on Ingrid Mårdh's foundational work while adding a pragmatic perspective that views headlinese syntax as optimized for reader engagement rather than purely space-saving.

## Relevance to Current Research

This work is foundational for understanding:
- The systematic nature of morphosyntactic transformations in headlines
- Why headlines constitute a distinct register rather than simplified text
- The pragmatic motivations behind syntactic deviations
- The persistence of headline grammar across media types

## Methodology

The study employs syntactic analysis of a corpus of English newspaper headlines, focusing on determiners and verb tenses as case studies for demonstrating the systematic nature of headlinese grammar.
